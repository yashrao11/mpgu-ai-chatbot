About MPGU and institutional structure The site structure page shows that MPGU is organized around a broad institutional model. Its major structural groups include University Management and Boards, Administration and University Branches, Institutes and Faculties, Center for Student Disability Services, University Life, and the Psychological Assistance Center. That structure matters for RAG because many student questions map directly to one of those institutional units. Admissions for foreign citizens The admissions page for foreign citizens is one of the most useful pages on the site. It gives a practical step-by-step path for international applicants and includes the official e-mail used to start document submission. The page also links to the personal-account instructions and the educational-program list for foreign citizens and stateless persons. Admission flow summarized G Step 1: choose an educational program from the official list. G Step 2: review the tuition cost for the selected level of study. G Step 3: prepare scans of the required documents and upload them to the applicant personal account. G Step 4: send additional materials if the selected program requires them (for example for music, art, or postgraduate study). G Step 5: wait for official acceptance and then follow the financing route (Rossotrudnichestvo or contract study). Core documents named on the page G Passport page with photo G Notarized Russian translation of the passport G Education document with legalization or apostille, plus notarized Russian translation G Photo 3x4 The page also states that applicants under contract should take entrance tests, sign a contract after successful results, pay tuition, and then contact the faculty to begin studies. For foreign-student questions, the page prominently uses the address foreign@mpgu.su and provides contact details for faculty curators. 4. Education at MPGU The education section is divided into bachelor, master, PhD, and the English and German program cluster. This gives the site a clear academic hierarchy, which is very useful for a structured university knowledge base. Bachelor level The bachelor page explains that the bachelor degree is the first step of higher education. It lasts four years in the standard case, and the page lists study calendars, degree outcomes, and progression options to master's study, short programs, or Russian language proficiency courses. G Typical duration: 4 years G Winter semester and summer semester dates are published on the page G Graduates receive a bachelor diploma G Students can continue to master's study or short-term programs The bachelor catalog is broad and is grouped by institutes and faculties. The visible examples on the page include geography, biology, childhood and defectology, journalism, arts, design, music, and other pedagogical tracks. Master level The master page explains that the master degree is the second step of higher education. The standard duration is two years, and graduates can continue to PhD study, short educational programs, or Russian language training. PhD level The PhD page explains that doctoral study is the third level of higher education in Russia. It emphasizes research, thesis work, and oral examination. The page states that completion normally takes three to four years depending on the study mode and program.
